Predicting Vehicle Prices in the California Market

A statistical Machine Learning Study by Sahand Shafieha

The Big Picture

We trained four independent Random Forest models — one per market tier — on 3,800 California used car listings. Each model was evaluated on a held-out test set using RMSE, MAE, MAPE, and R².

Analysis & Insights

This interactive forecast projects the 90-day market value decay for Affordable, Mid-Range, and Luxury tiers. It was obtained by passing simulated vehicle attributes (accumulating 40 miles/day) into our trained Random Forest Regressor. This contributed to our study by providing clear, category-specific depreciation schedules, giving buyers and sellers a concrete, data-driven tool to time transactions and evaluate total ownership costs over a 3-month horizon.

The Science Behind It

Cleaning & Imputation

Missing data is a common artifact of API scraping. We used K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) Imputation to fill in gaps for mileage and price. By comparing similar vehicles, we ensured our dataset remained robust without losing valuable rows of data.

Market Stratification

Cars aren't all the same. We split the data into Affordable, Mid-Range, and Luxury tiers to better understand how different classes of vehicles depreciate.

Random Forest Engine

Our predictions are powered by a Random Forest Regressor. It looks at hundreds of decision trees to find the most accurate price based on mileage and market demand.

Market Insights

Analysis & Insights

This plot illustrates the strong inverse relationship between mileage and listing price. It was obtained by mapping actual vehicle odometer readings against listing prices, grouped by affordability tier and fitted with linear regression trendlines. This contributed to our study by confirming mileage as the single most powerful individual predictor of vehicle depreciation, justifying its use as the core feature in our Random Forest regression model.

Analysis & Insights

This timeline tracks how average used car listing prices fluctuated day-to-day. It was obtained by grouping historical listing prices by their last seen date and computing a LOESS smoothed trendline for each affordability tier. This contributed to our study by demonstrating overall market stability during our data collection window, ensuring our baseline forecast wasn't skewed by sudden, highly volatile external market shocks.

Analysis & Insights

This scatter plot examines the relationship between price levels and inventory listing duration. It was obtained by plotting active vehicle prices against their total Days on Market (DOM), fitted with linear trendlines. This contributed to our study by revealing a distinct downward price pressure as cars sit longer, acting as a crucial proxy for seller motivation that increased our model's final predictive precision.

Analysis & Insights

This boxplot illustrates the overall price range, spread, and median for our three vehicle affordability segments. It was obtained by calculating the five-number summary (minimum, first quartile, median, third quartile, maximum) of listing prices grouped by affordability tier. This contributed to our study by validating our tier boundary selections ($30k and $45k) and confirming that our categorization successfully captured separate, non-overlapping price regimes.

Final Summary

Our analysis provides a clear window into the California used car market. By combining age, mileage, and market demand into a single predictive model, we help consumers make smarter buying and selling decisions.
